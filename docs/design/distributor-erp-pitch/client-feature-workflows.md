# Distributor ERP Feature List and Workflows

## Who this is for

This ERP is a strong fit for a distribution business that wants tighter control across sales, stock, dispatch, invoicing, collections, purchasing, and finance.

It is designed to help the business run daily operations from one connected system instead of managing the same transaction in separate sheets, chats, and follow-ups.

## What the ERP covers

### 1. Customer and dealer management

The ERP supports the full customer account setup needed for a distributor.

Features include:

- customer and dealer master creation
- account status control
- payment terms setup
- credit limit setup
- customer contact and commercial details
- dealer account listing and search
- support for dealer portal access

Why it matters:

The sales team starts with a clean customer account, finance has the right control points, and the business can manage repeat orders with less confusion.

### 2. Sales and order management

The ERP supports the full order lifecycle from order entry to dispatch readiness.

Features include:

- sales order creation
- draft, confirm, hold, cancel, and close states
- order timeline and status tracking
- product and quantity validation
- sales order search and filtering
- sales targets and promotion foundation

Why it matters:

Orders move through a controlled process instead of being managed informally through calls and manual updates.

### 3. Credit control and approval flow

The ERP includes credit-aware order control, which is important for distributors selling on credit.

Features include:

- credit limit setup per customer
- credit limit increase requests
- temporary credit override requests
- approval and rejection flow for credit decisions
- dunning hold support for risky accounts
- customer balance and exposure visibility

Why it matters:

The business can stop risky orders earlier, protect cash flow, and give sales teams a proper approval path when exceptions are needed.

### 4. Inventory and stock control

The ERP manages both finished goods and raw material stock, with batch visibility and stock movement tracking.

Features include:

- stock summary by item
- available, reserved, and on-hand stock visibility
- low stock alerts
- batch-level traceability
- stock adjustments
- opening stock import
- movement history
- expiring batch visibility

Why it matters:

Warehouse and sales teams can work from the same stock picture, and finance can trust the stock numbers more easily.

### 5. Dispatch and warehouse execution

The ERP supports the warehouse side of outbound order execution.

Features include:

- dispatch preparation
- pending dispatch queue
- dispatch preview
- dispatch confirmation
- delivery challan generation
- slip and order dispatch tracking
- backorder cancellation support

Why it matters:

The handoff from sales to warehouse becomes clearer, and dispatch activity is easier to track.

### 6. Invoicing and receivables

The ERP connects invoicing to actual dispatch activity and supports receivables follow-up.

Features include:

- invoice generation after dispatch
- invoice listing and detail
- invoice PDF access
- ledger visibility
- invoice aging
- outstanding balance tracking
- partial and full settlement support

Why it matters:

Finance and collections work from the same commercial truth as warehouse and sales, which reduces balance disputes and manual reconciliation.

### 7. Collections and customer settlement

The ERP supports receipt recording and settlement against open invoices.

Features include:

- payment recording
- allocation against invoices
- partial payment handling
- paid and part-paid invoice visibility
- customer statement access
- receivable aging support
- collection follow-up visibility

Why it matters:

Collections become easier to manage because the team can see what is open, what is partly paid, and what needs follow-up.

### 8. Purchasing and supplier management

The ERP supports the full buying process for stock and raw materials.

Features include:

- supplier master setup
- supplier approval and activation
- purchase order creation and approval
- goods receipt note entry
- purchase invoice capture
- purchase return handling
- supplier statement and aging
- supplier settlement support

Why it matters:

The purchasing team can track buying activity properly, and finance gets a cleaner payable picture.

### 9. Accounting and finance control

The ERP includes a real accounting layer, not just operational screens.

Features include:

- chart of accounts
- journals and postings
- receivable and payable ledger support
- bank reconciliation
- sub-ledger reconciliation
- GST reconciliation
- opening balance import
- period lock and period close
- correction through reversal entries

Why it matters:

Finance can move toward a cleaner close because commercial, stock, and accounting activity stay connected.

### 10. Reports and business visibility

The ERP includes operational and financial reporting for day-to-day control and month-end review.

Features include:

- trial balance
- profit and loss
- balance sheet
- cash flow
- GST reports
- inventory valuation
- inventory reconciliation
- aged receivables
- customer and supplier statements
- export request and download flow

Why it matters:

Management gets a clearer picture of stock, sales, receivables, payables, and finance performance without depending on scattered reports.

### 11. Admin, control, and audit support

The ERP includes the control surfaces needed to run the system safely.

Features include:

- user and role management
- company and tenant setup
- module access control
- support ticket handling
- export approval flow
- audit trail support
- runtime and feature control points

Why it matters:

The business can control who can do what, review sensitive actions, and keep the system governed as usage grows.

### 12. Dealer self-service portal

The ERP already supports dealer-facing self-service for selected commercial and finance tasks.

Features include:

- dealer dashboard
- order visibility
- invoice visibility
- aging view
- ledger access
- invoice PDF download
- credit limit request
- support ticket creation

Why it matters:

Customers can answer common questions themselves instead of depending on constant back-and-forth with the internal team.

## Core business workflows

### Workflow 1: Customer onboarding to first order

1. Create the customer or dealer account.
2. Set payment terms, credit limit, and commercial details.
3. Make the account active for sales use.
4. Let the sales team create the first order.

Business outcome:

The customer starts with a properly controlled account instead of an informal setup.

### Workflow 2: Order to dispatch

1. Sales creates the order.
2. The system checks customer status, credit, and product validity.
3. Stock is checked and reserved where applicable.
4. The order is confirmed.
5. Warehouse prepares the dispatch.
6. Dispatch is confirmed in the system.

Business outcome:

Sales, warehouse, and finance all work from the same order status.

### Workflow 3: Dispatch to invoice

1. Dispatch is completed.
2. The invoice is generated from actual shipment activity.
3. The receivable is created and tracked.
4. The customer can see the invoice and balance.

Business outcome:

Invoice timing is easier to trust because it follows the real dispatch event.

### Workflow 4: Payment to settlement

1. The business records payment received from the customer.
2. Payment is allocated to one or more invoices.
3. Open balances update automatically.
4. The team can see which invoices are paid, part-paid, or still pending.

Business outcome:

Collections and receivables follow-up become more structured.

### Workflow 5: Supplier purchase to stock intake

1. Create and approve the supplier.
2. Create the purchase order.
3. Receive goods through GRN.
4. Capture the supplier invoice.
5. Track payables and settle supplier dues.

Business outcome:

Buying, stock intake, and supplier finance stay linked.

### Workflow 6: Stock control and traceability

1. Monitor stock by item and batch.
2. Track low stock and expiring stock.
3. Adjust stock when needed with traceable records.
4. Review movement history for investigation or control.

Business outcome:

The business can trust stock movements and investigate issues faster.

### Workflow 7: Finance close and reporting

1. Review journals and ledgers.
2. Reconcile banks, receivables, and payables.
3. Review GST and closing checks.
4. Lock or close the period.
5. Export reports for review.

Business outcome:

Month-end becomes less of a repair exercise and more of a controlled close.

## Best fit for a distributor

For a distribution company, the strongest value is in these connected areas:

- customer and dealer management
- credit-controlled order processing
- stock visibility and dispatch control
- invoice and receivable visibility
- collections follow-up
- supplier buying and stock intake
- accounting and close discipline
- reporting for management review

## Optional expansion areas

The ERP also includes areas that may matter later depending on how the business operates:

- factory and production workflows for businesses that do repacking, blending, or light manufacturing
- HR and payroll for internal workforce management
- broader portal and admin control for multi-team operations

## Short version you can send to a client

This ERP is built for a distribution business that wants better control from order booking to dispatch, invoicing, collections, purchasing, stock control, and finance close.

It helps the business manage customers, sales orders, credit approvals, inventory, dispatch, invoicing, receivables, supplier purchases, accounting, reporting, and dealer self-service from one connected system.
